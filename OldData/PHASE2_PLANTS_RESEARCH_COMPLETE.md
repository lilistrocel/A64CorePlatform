## 1. EGGPLANT (Solanum melongena)

### Basic Information
- **Scientific Name:** Solanum melongena L.
- **Common Names:** Eggplant, Aubergine, Brinjal
- **Family:** Solanaceae
- **Farm Type Compatibility:** open_field, greenhouse, hydroponic

### Growth Cycle
- **Germination:** 7-10 days (70-90°F/21-32°C optimal)
- **Transplant Age:** 6-8 weeks
- **Days to Harvest:** 65-85 days from transplanting
- **Total Cycle:** 100-120 days from seed
- **Flowering to Harvest:** 3 weeks

### Yield Information
- **Yield Per Plant:** 4-6 kg
- **Expected Waste:** 15%
- **Commercial Yield:** 121 t/ha with optimal fertilization

### Fertilizer Schedule
**Pre-plant (per 100 sq ft):**
- N: 75-120 lb/acre
- P2O5: 100-150 lb/acre
- K2O: 50-150 lb/acre
- S: 20-30 lb/acre

**Side-dress:** 30-50 lb N/acre after first flowers set

**NPK Ratios:** Similar to tomatoes - balanced 5-5-5 initially, then higher potassium for fruiting

### Hydroponic Parameters
- **pH:** 5.5-6.5
- **EC:** 2.5-3.5 mS/cm (vary by stage)
  - Seedlings: 2.5 mS/cm
  - Vegetative: 3.0 mS/cm
  - Fruiting: 3.5 mS/cm
- **Temperature:** 70-85°F (21-29°C)
- **Humidity:** 40-70% (prefer 40-60%)
- **Light:** 12-16 hours, DLI 20-30 mol/m²/day
- **Spacing:** 16-24 inches (40-60 cm), 3-5 plants/m²

### Common Pests & Diseases
1. **Flea Beetles** (HIGH severity)
   - Symptoms: Small shot-holes in leaves
   - Prevention: Floating row covers on young plants
   - Treatment: Spinosad, permethrin, carbaryl

2. **Aphids** (MEDIUM severity)
   - Symptoms: Leaf curling, honeydew, virus vectors
   - Prevention: Beneficial insects, avoid excess nitrogen
   - Treatment: Insecticidal soap, neem oil

3. **Spider Mites** (MEDIUM severity)
   - Symptoms: Yellow stippling, webbing
   - Prevention: Increase humidity, predatory mites
   - Treatment: Miticide spray

4. **Verticillium Wilt** (HIGH severity)
   - Symptoms: Wilting, yellowing, vascular discoloration
   - Prevention: Resistant varieties, crop rotation
   - Treatment: No cure - remove infected plants

5. **Blossom End Rot** (MEDIUM severity)
   - Symptoms: Dark sunken spots on fruit end
   - Prevention: Consistent moisture, calcium
   - Treatment: Improve irrigation, foliar calcium

### IPM Strategies
- Row covers for flea beetles
- Attract beneficial insects (parasitic wasps, lacewings)
- Proper fertilization per soil test
- Water at base, avoid wetting foliage
- Remove weeds before flowering

### Companion Planting
- **Beneficial:** Beans, Marigold, Peas, Spinach, Thyme
- **Incompatible:** Peppers, Potatoes (same family - disease sharing)

### UAE Adaptations
- **Season:** Warm season - March to October
- **Planting Months:** March, April, May
- **Harvest Months:** June, July, August, September, October
- **Heat Tolerance:** HIGH (loves heat 75-95°F)
- **Water Requirement:** HIGH
- **Greenhouse:** Optional for extreme heat (>40°C)
- **Notes:** "Excellent for UAE! Heat-loving crop that thrives in summer. Popular in Middle Eastern and Asian cuisine. Requires consistent watering in desert climate. Well-suited for greenhouse or outdoor with shade cloth."

### Economics
- **Market Price:** AED 8-12/kg
- **Labor Hours:** 1.5 hours/plant over full cycle

### Quality Grading
- **Grade A:** Firm, glossy skin, uniform color, 300-400g, no blemishes
- **Grade B:** Slight dullness acceptable, 200-300g, minor marks OK

## 2. OKRA (Abelmoschus esculentus)

### Basic Information
- **Scientific Name:** Abelmoschus esculentus
- **Common Names:** Okra, Lady's Finger, Bhindi, Bamia
- **Family:** Malvaceae
- **Farm Type Compatibility:** open_field, greenhouse, hydroponic

### Growth Cycle
- **Germination:** 7-14 days (70-95°F optimal)
- **Days to Harvest:** 50-70 days
- **Harvest Frequency:** Every 2 days (very fast-growing)
- **Total Cycle:** 60-75 days

### Yield Information
- **Yield Per Plant:** 2-3 kg (with continuous harvest)
- **Pods Per Plant:** 30-40 pods/week with 5-10 plants
- **Expected Waste:** 10%

### Fertilizer Schedule
**Initial (4-6 weeks):**
- NPK 5-10-10

**Ongoing:**
- Balanced liquid fertilizer monthly, OR
- Side dress with 10-10-10, aged manure, compost

**Ideal Ratios:** 1:2 N:K ratio encourages flowers
- Examples: 4-0-8, 5-0-10, 10-0-20

**UAE Optimal (at pod development):**
- N: 18.2-27.3 kg/acre
- P: 22.8-36.4 kg/acre
- K: 109.2-182 kg/acre
- S: 9.1-18.2 kg/acre
- Zn: 0.9-2.7 kg/acre

### Hydroponic Parameters
- **pH:** 6.0-6.5
- **EC:** 2.0-2.4 mS/cm
- **PPM:** 1400-1680
- **Temperature:** 75-95°F (optimal growth)
- **Germination Temp:** 75°F
- **Spacing:** 12-18 inches apart, rows 3 feet apart
- **Water:** 1 inch/week

### Common Pests & Diseases
1. **Aphids** (MEDIUM severity)
   - Multiple species (cotton aphid most common)
   - Treatment: Neem oil, insecticidal soap

2. **Powdery Mildew** (HIGH severity)
   - Caused by Golovinomyces cichoracearum
   - Symptoms: White powdery spots on leaves/stems
   - Prevention: Good air circulation, avoid overhead watering
   - Treatment: Sulfur fungicides, neem oil

3. **Flea Beetles** (MEDIUM severity)
   - Symptoms: Small holes in leaves
   - Prevention: Row covers
   - Treatment: Pyrethrin sprays

4. **Other Pests:** Cotton bollworm, whitefly, spider mites, leaf roller

5. **Other Diseases:** Fusarium wilt, Verticillium wilt, Cercospora leaf spot, damping-off

### Companion Planting
- **Beneficial:** Peppers, Cucumbers, Melons, Basil
- **Incompatible:** (None specifically noted)

### UAE Adaptations
- **Season:** PERFECT FOR UAE SUMMER! May-November
- **Planting Months:** April, May, June, July
- **Harvest Months:** May, June, July, August, September, October, November
- **Heat Tolerance:** VERY HIGH (75-95°F optimal - loves UAE heat!)
- **Water Requirement:** MEDIUM-HIGH (1 inch/week)
- **Greenhouse:** Not needed - thrives in open field
- **Traditional Crop:** YES - widely grown in UAE, Abu Dhabi, Dubai
- **Notes:** "EXCELLENT for UAE summer production! Traditional Middle Eastern vegetable with strong local demand. Extremely heat-tolerant. Harvest every 2 days for continuous production. Very fast ROI. Popular in local cuisine (bamia stew). Can be grown hydroponically for water efficiency."

### Economics
- **Market Price:** AED 10-15/kg (high demand in UAE)
- **Labor Hours:** 0.8 hours/plant (frequent harvest required)

### Quality Grading
- **Grade A:** 3-5 inches long, tender, bright green, no damage
- **Grade B:** 5-7 inches, slightly tougher, minor blemishes OK

## 3. ZUCCHINI (Cucurbita pepo)

### Basic Information
- **Scientific Name:** Cucurbita pepo L.
- **Common Names:** Zucchini, Courgette, Summer Squash
- **Family:** Cucurbitaceae
- **Farm Type Compatibility:** open_field, greenhouse, hydroponic
- **Varieties:** Green Zucchini, Yellow Zucchini (similar cultivation)

### Growth Cycle
- **Germination:** 7-14 days (70°F/21°C optimal, min 60°F)
- **Transplant:** Sow indoors 3-4 weeks before last frost
- **Days to Harvest:** 40-50 days from transplant
- **Total Cycle:** 55-70 days from seed
- **Greenhouse Cycle:** 120-150 days

### Yield Information
- **Yield Per Plant:** 3-5 kg (bush varieties)
- **Commercial Greenhouse:** 30-35 tons/acre
- **Expected Waste:** 15%

### Fertilizer Schedule
**Initial:**
- High nitrogen and phosphorus at beginning of cycle
- NPK 8-16-36 recommended

**During Growth:**
- Start with low potassium
- Gradually increase K to maximum by harvest
- Emphasize potassium application at fruiting

**Ratios:**
- Early: Higher N-P for vegetative growth
- Fruiting: Emphasize K for fruit development

### Hydroponic Parameters
- **pH:** 6.0-6.5
- **EC:** 2.5-4.0 mS/cm (substrate), 3.0-3.5 mS/cm (solution)
  - Varies by season and light availability
- **Temperature:** 70-80°F optimal
- **Medium:** Rock wool (most common), coconut coir, peat/perlite
- **Spacing:** 1.5-1.7 plants/yard² (avoid overcrowding)
- **Light:** Similar to tomato/cucumber requirements

### Common Pests & Diseases
1. **Powdery Mildew** (HIGH severity)
   - Symptoms: White powdery spots on leaves/stems
   - Prevention: Good air circulation, resistant varieties
   - Treatment: Fungicides, remove infected leaves

2. **Aphids** (MEDIUM severity)
   - Symptoms: Leaf curling, yellowing, virus transmission
   - Prevention: Reflective mulches, row covers
   - Treatment: Insecticidal soap, neem oil, beneficial insects

3. **Cucumber Beetles** (HIGH severity)
   - Two types: Spotted (12 black spots) and Striped (3 black stripes)
   - Transmit: Bacterial wilt, squash mosaic virus
   - Prevention: Row covers, reflective mulch
   - Treatment: Pyrethrins, neem oil (evening application)

4. **Squash Vine Borers** (HIGH severity)
   - Symptoms: Wilting vines, sawdust-like frass at stem base
   - Prevention: Row covers, crop rotation
   - Treatment: Cut open stem to remove larvae

### IPM Strategies
- **Floating row covers** at planting (remove for pollination)
- **Crop rotation:** 3-4 year cycle
- **Sanitation:** Remove old plants after harvest
- **Reflective mulches** deter aphids/beetles
- **Biological control:** Lady beetles, lacewings, ground beetles
- **Late evening spraying** to protect pollinators

### Companion Planting
- **Beneficial:** Beans, Corn, Peas, Radish, Marigold, Nasturtium
- **Incompatible:** Potatoes

### UAE Adaptations
- **Season:** Cool to warm season - February to May, September to November
- **Planting Months:** February, March, April, September, October
- **Harvest Months:** March, April, May, June, October, November, December
- **Heat Tolerance:** MEDIUM (prefers 70-80°F, struggles >95°F)
- **Water Requirement:** HIGH (consistent moisture critical)
- **Greenhouse:** RECOMMENDED for UAE - climate control extends season
- **Notes:** "Greenhouse production recommended for UAE due to heat sensitivity. Very fast-growing and productive. Harvest at 3-8 inches for best quality. Popular in local markets. Climate control essential for summer production. Training system in greenhouse improves yields."

### Economics
- **Market Price:** AED 8-12/kg
- **Labor Hours:** 0.6 hours/plant

### Quality Grading
- **Grade A:** 6-8 inches (15-20cm), firm, glossy, uniform color, no damage
- **Grade B:** 8-10 inches, slight surface marks OK, good firmness
