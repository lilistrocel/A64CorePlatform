## Plant 31: Bean - Green (Bush Bean)

**Scientific Name:** Phaseolus vulgaris L.

### Botanical Information
- **Family:** Fabaceae (Legume family)
- **Type:** Annual, warm-season vegetable
- **Growth Habit:** Bush type (determinate) or pole type (indeterminate)
- **USDA Verification:** Phaseolus vulgaris L. - Common Bean

### Growth Cycle
- **Germination:** 7-14 days (soil temp 60-85°F / 16-29°C)
- **Vegetative Stage:** 21-28 days
- **Flowering Stage:** 7-10 days
- **Fruiting/Pod Development:** 14-21 days
- **Harvest Duration:** 14-21 days (multiple harvests)
- **Total Cycle:** 56-70 days (bush), 70-90 days (pole)

### Yield
- **Yield per Plant:** 0.5-1.0 kg (bush), 1.5-2.5 kg (pole)
- **Waste:** 10% (stem ends trimmed)

### Hydroponic Requirements
- **EC:** 2.0-4.0 mS/cm (1400-2800 ppm)
- **pH:** 6.0-6.5
- **Temperature:** Water 18-24°C, Air 21-27°C
- **Light:** High (DLI 14-18, PPFD 400-600)
- **Water:** 1.5-2.0 L/day per plant

### Fertilizer Schedule (NPK)
- **Germination:** 5-5-5 (weekly)
- **Vegetative:** 10-10-10 (weekly) - LOW nitrogen! Beans fix N
- **Flowering:** 5-15-15 (weekly) - boost P&K for pods
- **Fruiting:** 5-20-20 (weekly) - high K for pod filling

### Pests (UC Davis IPM)
1. **Aphids** (Bean aphid, Cowpea aphid)
   - Severity: Medium
   - Organic: Insecticidal soap, neem oil
   - Biological: Ladybugs, lacewings, Aphidius wasps
   - Cultural: Remove infested plants, control ants

2. **Spider Mites**
   - Severity: Medium (in hot dry conditions)
   - Organic: Strong water spray, insecticidal soap
   - Biological: Predatory mites (Phytoseiulus)
   - Cultural: Increase humidity, avoid water stress

3. **Bean Leaf Beetle**
   - Severity: Low
   - Organic: Neem oil, pyrethrin
   - Biological: None specific
   - Cultural: Remove crop debris, rotate crops

### Diseases (UC Davis IPM)
1. **Bean Common Mosaic Virus** (transmitted by aphids)
   - Severity: High
   - Symptoms: Mottled yellow-green leaves, stunted growth
   - Organic: Remove infected plants immediately
   - Prevention: Use certified virus-free seed, control aphids early, avoid touching wet plants

2. **Powdery Mildew**
   - Severity: Medium
   - Symptoms: White powdery coating on leaves
   - Organic: Sulfur spray, potassium bicarbonate
   - Prevention: Good air circulation, avoid overhead watering

3. **Root Rot** (Pythium, Rhizoctonia)
   - Severity: Medium
   - Symptoms: Wilting, brown mushy roots
   - Organic: Biological fungicides (Trichoderma)
   - Prevention: Well-drained media, avoid overwatering

### Environmental Requirements
- **Temperature:** Min 15°C, Max 32°C, Optimal 24°C
- **Humidity:** 50-70%, Optimal 60%
- **Light Hours:** 12-14 hours
- **CO2:** 800-1000 ppm
- **Soil pH:** 6.0-7.0

### UAE Adaptations
- **Heat Tolerance:** MEDIUM - Tolerates warm temperatures but suffers >35°C
- **Cold Tolerance:** VERY LOW - Frost-sensitive
- **Water Requirement:** MEDIUM-HIGH (1.5-2.0 L/day)
- **Greenhouse Required:** Yes in summer (May-Sep), No in winter (Oct-Apr)
- **Summer Outdoor:** NOT suitable (too hot >35°C, flowers abort)
- **Winter Outdoor:** EXCELLENT (Oct-Apr, cool weather ideal)
- **Best Planting Months:** October, November, December, January, February, March
- **Local Challenges:** Summer heat causes flower drop and poor pod set; requires shade or greenhouse cooling
- **Local Recommendations:** Grow Oct-Apr outdoors for best yields; May-Sep only in cooled greenhouse; popular vegetable with steady local demand; fast crop (60 days); nitrogen-fixing reduces fertilizer costs

### Companion Plants
- **Good:** Corn, Celery, Cucumber, Potato, Carrot, Radish
- **Bad:** Onion, Garlic, Fennel
- **Reasoning:** Legumes fix nitrogen benefiting heavy feeders; alliums stunt bean growth; fennel inhibits most vegetables

### Economics
- **Market Price:** 10-15 AED/kg
- **Demand:** HIGH (steady year-round demand)
- **Shelf Life:** 7-10 days (refrigerated 7°C, high humidity)
- **Storage:** Refrigerate immediately, high humidity (95%)

### Quality Grading
- **Premium:** Straight pods 10-15 cm, tender, bright green, no seeds visible, crisp snap
- **Standard:** Good pod shape, minor blemishes ok, slightly larger seeds ok
- **Reject:** Tough fibrous pods, bulging seeds, yellowing, disease

### Sources
- UC Davis IPM: Bean Pest Management Guidelines
- NC State Extension: Bush Bean Production
- USDA PLANTS: Phaseolus vulgaris verification
- Hydroponic research: EC/pH requirements for legumes

## Plant 32: Bean - Long / Yard Long Bean

**Scientific Name:** Vigna unguiculata subsp. sesquipedalis (L.) Verdc.

### Botanical Information
- **Family:** Fabaceae (Legume family)
- **Type:** Annual, warm-season vegetable
- **Common Names:** Asparagus bean, Chinese long bean, Snake bean, Dow Gauk
- **Growth Habit:** Vining/climbing (requires trellis)
- **USDA Verification:** Vigna unguiculata subsp. sesquipedalis

### Growth Cycle
- **Germination:** 7-10 days (soil temp 25-35°C)
- **Vegetative Stage:** 28-35 days
- **Flowering Stage:** 10-14 days
- **Fruiting/Pod Development:** 21-28 days
- **Harvest Duration:** 30-45 days (multiple harvests)
- **Total Cycle:** 70-90 days

### Yield
- **Yield per Plant:** 2.0-3.5 kg per plant (vining type, multiple harvests)
- **Waste:** 10% (ends trimmed)

### Hydroponic Requirements
- **EC:** 2.0-3.5 mS/cm (1400-2450 ppm)
- **pH:** 5.5-6.5
- **Temperature:** Water 22-28°C, Air 27-32°C
- **Light:** High (DLI 16-20, PPFD 450-650)
- **Water:** 2.0-3.0 L/day per plant (high transpiration due to large leaf area)

### Fertilizer Schedule (NPK)
- **Germination:** 5-5-5 (weekly)
- **Vegetative:** 10-10-10 (bi-weekly) - LOW nitrogen, beans fix N
- **Flowering:** 5-15-15 (weekly)
- **Fruiting:** 5-20-20 (weekly) - high K for long pod development

### Pests
1. **Aphids**
   - Severity: Low-Medium
   - Organic: Insecticidal soap, neem oil
   - Biological: Ladybugs, lacewings
   - Cultural: Monitor new growth

2. **Pod Borers** (Maruca vitrata)
   - Severity: Medium-High
   - Organic: Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis), neem oil
   - Biological: Trichogramma wasps
   - Cultural: Remove infested pods, pheromone traps

3. **Whiteflies**
   - Severity: Low
   - Organic: Insecticidal soap, yellow sticky traps
   - Biological: Encarsia formosa (parasitic wasp)
   - Cultural: Remove lower leaves, maintain airflow

### Diseases
1. **Rust** (Uromyces appendiculatus)
   - Severity: Medium
   - Symptoms: Orange-brown pustules on leaves
   - Organic: Sulfur spray, copper fungicide
   - Prevention: Good air circulation, avoid overhead watering

2. **Anthracnose**
   - Severity: Medium
   - Symptoms: Dark sunken lesions on pods and stems
   - Organic: Copper fungicide, remove infected plants
   - Prevention: Use disease-free seed, avoid working with wet plants

3. **Root Rot**
   - Severity: Low-Medium
   - Symptoms: Wilting, yellowing, brown roots
   - Organic: Biological fungicides
   - Prevention: Well-drained media, avoid overwatering

### Environmental Requirements
- **Temperature:** Min 20°C, Max 38°C, Optimal 30°C
- **Humidity:** 50-80%, Optimal 65%
- **Light Hours:** 12-14 hours
- **CO2:** 800-1000 ppm
- **Soil pH:** 5.5-7.0

### UAE Adaptations
- **Heat Tolerance:** VERY HIGH - Thrives in midsummer heat 27-38°C!
- **Cold Tolerance:** VERY LOW - Frost-sensitive, damaged <15°C
- **Water Requirement:** HIGH (2-3 L/day in summer)
- **Greenhouse Required:** No (LOVES UAE summer heat!)
- **Summer Outdoor:** EXCELLENT (Apr-Oct, peak production in hottest months!)
- **Winter Outdoor:** NOT suitable (too cold Nov-Feb, slows growth)
- **Best Planting Months:** March, April, May, June, July, August, September
- **Local Challenges:** Requires tall trellis (2-3 m); pods must be harvested young (30-40 cm) before they get tough
- **Local Recommendations:** PERFECT for UAE summer production when other beans fail! Heat-loving tropical bean; highly popular in Asian cuisine with large UAE demand; continuous harvest for 6-8 weeks; excellent summer crop for commercial farms

### Companion Plants
- **Good:** Corn, Cucumber, Eggplant, Radish
- **Bad:** Onion, Garlic, Fennel
- **Reasoning:** Benefits from tall support crops; alliums inhibit growth; fennel allelopathic

### Economics
- **Market Price:** 12-18 AED/kg
- **Demand:** VERY HIGH (large Asian expat population in UAE)
- **Shelf Life:** 7-10 days (refrigerated 7-10°C, high humidity)
- **Storage:** Refrigerate immediately, high humidity (95%)

### Quality Grading
- **Premium:** Long pods 30-40 cm, pencil-thin, tender, bright green, no seeds visible, crisp
- **Standard:** Pods 40-50 cm, slightly thicker, minor blemishes ok
- **Reject:** Tough fibrous pods, bulging seeds, yellowing, >50 cm (too tough)

### Sources
- USDA PLANTS: Vigna unguiculata verification
- NC State Extension: Asparagus Bean Culture
- Epic Gardening: Yardlong Bean Growing Guide

## Plant 33: Cabbage - Round (Green Cabbage)

**Scientific Name:** Brassica oleracea var. capitata L.

### Botanical Information
- **Family:** Brassicaceae (Mustard family)
- **Type:** Biennial grown as annual
- **Common Names:** Green cabbage, Round cabbage, Headed cabbage
- **Growth Habit:** Rosette forming tight head
- **USDA Verification:** Brassica oleracea var. capitata

### Growth Cycle
- **Germination:** 7-10 days
- **Vegetative Stage:** 60-80 days (head formation)
- **Flowering Stage:** 0 (harvested before flowering)
- **Fruiting Stage:** 0
- **Harvest Duration:** 7-14 days
- **Total Cycle:** 70-100 days (depends on variety)

### Yield
- **Yield per Plant:** 1.0-2.5 kg per head
- **Waste:** 20% (outer leaves removed)

### Hydroponic Requirements
- **EC:** 2.5-3.0 mS/cm (1750-2100 ppm)
- **pH:** 6.0-6.5
- **Temperature:** Water 16-20°C, Air 15-20°C (cool season crop)
- **Light:** Medium-High (DLI 14-17, PPFD 300-450)
- **Water:** 2.0-3.0 L/day per plant (large leaves, high transpiration)

### Fertilizer Schedule (NPK)
- **Germination:** 5-5-5 (weekly)
- **Vegetative:** 15-5-10 (weekly) - HIGH nitrogen for leaf growth
- **Head Formation:** 10-10-15 (weekly) - balanced for tight heads
- **Final Stage:** 5-10-15 (bi-weekly) - reduce N, increase K

### Pests (UC Davis IPM)
1. **Cabbage Aphid** (Brevicoryne brassicae)
   - Severity: High
   - Organic: Insecticidal soap, neem oil, pyrethrin
   - Biological: Ladybugs, lacewings, Aphidius wasps
   - Cultural: Remove infested plants, reflective mulch

2. **Cabbage Looper** (Trichoplusia ni)
   - Severity: High
   - Organic: Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis), Spinosad
   - Biological: Trichogramma wasps, parasitic flies
   - Cultural: Row covers, hand-pick larvae

3. **Flea Beetles**
   - Severity: Medium (especially on young plants)
   - Organic: Kaolin clay, neem oil
   - Biological: None specific
   - Cultural: Row covers on seedlings, trap crops

4. **Cabbage Root Maggot**
   - Severity: Medium-High
   - Organic: Beneficial nematodes
   - Biological: Rove beetles, ground beetles
   - Cultural: Row covers, collars around stems, avoid overfertilizing

### Diseases (UC Davis IPM)
1. **Clubroot** (Plasmodiophora brassicae)
   - Severity: Very High (soil-borne)
   - Symptoms: Swollen distorted roots, wilting, stunted growth
   - Organic: Raise pH to 7.2+, remove infected plants
   - Prevention: Use disease-free transplants, long crop rotation (7+ years), lime soil

2. **Black Rot** (Xanthomonas campestris)
   - Severity: High
   - Symptoms: V-shaped yellow lesions from leaf margins, black veins
   - Organic: Copper fungicide, remove infected plants
   - Prevention: Use clean seed, avoid overhead watering, destroy crop debris

3. **Downy Mildew**
   - Severity: Medium
   - Symptoms: Yellow patches on upper leaf surface, white mold underneath
   - Organic: Copper fungicide
   - Prevention: Good air circulation, reduce humidity

### Environmental Requirements
- **Temperature:** Min 5°C, Max 25°C, Optimal 18°C
- **Humidity:** 50-70%, Optimal 60%
- **Light Hours:** 12-16 hours
- **CO2:** 800-1000 ppm
- **Soil pH:** 6.0-7.5 (higher pH reduces clubroot)

### UAE Adaptations
- **Heat Tolerance:** VERY LOW - Cannot tolerate UAE summer heat
- **Cold Tolerance:** HIGH - Tolerates light frost, improved flavor
- **Water Requirement:** HIGH (2-3 L/day)
- **Greenhouse Required:** Yes (climate control for year-round)
- **Summer Outdoor:** NOT suitable (bolts rapidly >25°C)
- **Winter Outdoor:** EXCELLENT (Nov-Mar, cool season ideal)
- **Best Planting Months:** October, November, December, January, February
- **Local Challenges:** Requires cool temperatures; UAE summer too hot; clubroot risk in contaminated media; heavy feeder needing high nitrogen
- **Local Recommendations:** Grow only Nov-Mar outdoors or year-round in climate-controlled greenhouse (16-20°C); popular vegetable with steady demand; good storage crop; choose fast-maturing varieties (70-80 days) for UAE winter window

### Companion Plants
- **Good:** Onion, Celery, Beet, Potato, Dill (repels aphids)
- **Bad:** Strawberry, Tomato, Pole Beans
- **Reasoning:** Aromatic herbs repel cabbage pests; avoid heavy feeders competing for nutrients

### Economics
- **Market Price:** 4-6 AED/kg
- **Demand:** HIGH (steady year-round demand)
- **Shelf Life:** 60-90 days (refrigerated 0-2°C, high humidity 95-98%)
- **Storage:** Excellent storage crop, long shelf life

### Quality Grading
- **Premium:** Tight solid heads 1.5-2.5 kg, uniform green color, no splits, crisp texture
- **Standard:** Reasonably tight heads, minor outer leaf damage ok
- **Reject:** Loose heads, splitting, yellowing, insect damage, disease

### Sources
- UC Davis IPM: Cabbage Pest Management
- NC State Extension: Cabbage Production
- Cornell Vegetable Program: Brassica Culture
- USDA PLANTS: Brassica oleracea verification

## Plant 34: Cauliflower

**Scientific Name:** Brassica oleracea var. botrytis L.

### Botanical Information
- **Family:** Brassicaceae (Mustard family)
- **Type:** Biennial grown as annual
- **Common Names:** Cauliflower, Gobi (Hindi/Urdu)
- **Growth Habit:** Rosette with compact white curd (head)
- **USDA Verification:** Brassica oleracea var. botrytis

### Growth Cycle
- **Germination:** 7-10 days
- **Vegetative Stage:** 50-70 days (before curd formation)
- **Curd Formation:** 20-30 days (buttoning to mature head)
- **Flowering Stage:** 0 (harvested at curd stage)
- **Fruiting Stage:** 0
- **Harvest Duration:** 3-7 days (narrow harvest window!)
- **Total Cycle:** 75-100 days

### Yield
- **Yield per Plant:** 0.8-1.5 kg per head
- **Waste:** 40% (leaves, stem, outer curd)

### Hydroponic Requirements
- **EC:** 2.5-3.0 mS/cm (1750-2100 ppm)
- **pH:** 6.0-7.0
- **Temperature:** Water 16-20°C, Air 15-20°C (cool season, very temperature-sensitive!)
- **Light:** Medium-High (DLI 14-17, PPFD 300-450)
- **Water:** 2.5-3.5 L/day per plant (large leaves)

### Fertilizer Schedule (NPK)
- **Germination:** 5-5-5 (weekly)
- **Vegetative:** 15-5-10 (weekly) - HIGH nitrogen for leaf growth
- **Curd Initiation:** 10-10-15 (weekly) - balanced nutrition
- **Curd Development:** 5-15-20 (weekly) - reduce N, boost P&K for curd quality

### Pests (UC Davis IPM - similar to cabbage)
1. **Cabbage Aphid**
   - Severity: High
   - Organic: Insecticidal soap, neem oil
   - Biological: Ladybugs, Aphidius wasps
   - Cultural: Monitor regularly, remove infested leaves

2. **Cabbage Looper & Imported Cabbageworm**
   - Severity: High
   - Organic: Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis), Spinosad
   - Biological: Trichogramma wasps
   - Cultural: Row covers, inspect for eggs

3. **Flea Beetles**
   - Severity: Medium
   - Organic: Kaolin clay, neem oil
   - Biological: None specific
   - Cultural: Row covers on seedlings

4. **Thrips**
   - Severity: Medium (can cause brown spots on curd)
   - Organic: Spinosad, insecticidal soap
   - Biological: Predatory mites
   - Cultural: Blue sticky traps

### Diseases (UC Davis IPM)
1. **Clubroot** (Plasmodiophora brassicae)
   - Severity: Very High
   - Symptoms: Swollen roots, stunted plants, wilting
   - Organic: Raise pH to 7.2+, long rotation
   - Prevention: Clean media, avoid contaminated soil

2. **Black Rot** (Xanthomonas campestris)
   - Severity: High
   - Symptoms: V-shaped yellow lesions, black veins, curd discoloration
   - Organic: Copper fungicide, remove infected plants
   - Prevention: Clean seed, avoid overhead watering

3. **Downy Mildew**
   - Severity: Medium
   - Symptoms: Yellow patches on leaves, white mold
   - Organic: Copper fungicide
   - Prevention: Good air circulation, reduce humidity

4. **Curd Rot** (Bacterial soft rot)
   - Severity: Medium
   - Symptoms: Water-soaked soft rot on curd
   - Organic: Remove infected plants
   - Prevention: Avoid water on curd, good drainage

### Environmental Requirements
- **Temperature:** Min 5°C, Max 24°C, Optimal 16°C (VERY temperature-sensitive!)
- **Humidity:** 50-70%, Optimal 60%
- **Light Hours:** 12-16 hours
- **CO2:** 800-1000 ppm
- **Soil pH:** 6.5-7.5 (higher pH reduces clubroot)

### UAE Adaptations
- **Heat Tolerance:** VERY LOW - Extremely sensitive to heat stress
- **Cold Tolerance:** HIGH - Tolerates light frost
- **Water Requirement:** VERY HIGH (2.5-3.5 L/day)
- **Greenhouse Required:** Yes (precise climate control essential)
- **Summer Outdoor:** NOT suitable (buttons or no curd formation >24°C)
- **Winter Outdoor:** CHALLENGING (requires perfect cool weather Nov-Jan)
- **Best Planting Months:** November, December, January (narrow window!)
- **Local Challenges:** MOST DIFFICULT brassica! Temperature swings cause buttoning (tiny heads), uneven temps cause ricey/fuzzy curds, high heat prevents curd formation; requires precise 15-20°C range
- **Local Recommendations:** Only for experienced growers with climate-controlled greenhouses; choose fast-maturing varieties (60-70 days); self-blanching white varieties (leaves naturally cover curd); popular in UAE but challenging to grow; high value crop if successful; consider purple/orange varieties (more heat-tolerant, no blanching needed)

### Companion Plants
- **Good:** Onion, Celery, Beet, Dill
- **Bad:** Strawberry, Tomato
- **Reasoning:** Aromatic herbs deter pests; avoid heavy feeders

### Economics
- **Market Price:** 8-12 AED/kg
- **Demand:** VERY HIGH (popular in Middle Eastern, South Asian cuisine)
- **Shelf Life:** 14-21 days (refrigerated 0-2°C, high humidity 95%, plastic film)
- **Storage:** Moderate shelf life, sensitive to ethylene (causes yellowing)

### Quality Grading
- **Premium:** Tight compact white curd, 15-20 cm diameter, no discoloration, no ricey/fuzzy texture, clean
- **Standard:** Slight yellowing ok, minor blemishes, reasonably compact
- **Reject:** Ricey/fuzzy curd, severe discoloration, insect damage, disease, loose curd

### Sources
- UC Davis IPM: Cauliflower Pest Management
- Cornell Vegetable Program: Cauliflower Culture
- NC State Extension: Cauliflower Production
- Johnny's Seeds: Temperature management for quality curds

## Plant 35: Celery

**Scientific Name:** Apium graveolens L.

### Botanical Information
- **Family:** Apiaceae (Carrot family)
- **Type:** Biennial grown as annual
- **Common Names:** Celery, Garden celery
- **Growth Habit:** Rosette of upright petioles (stalks)
- **USDA Verification:** Apium graveolens L.

### Growth Cycle
- **Germination:** 14-21 days (slow! requires light)
- **Vegetative Stage:** 90-120 days
- **Flowering Stage:** 0 (harvested before bolting)
- **Fruiting Stage:** 0
- **Harvest Duration:** 7-14 days
- **Total Cycle:** 110-140 days (LONG season crop)

### Yield
- **Yield per Plant:** 0.8-1.2 kg per plant (10-15 stalks)
- **Waste:** 30% (leaves, base, stringy outer stalks)

### Hydroponic Requirements
- **EC:** 1.8-2.4 mS/cm (1260-1680 ppm)
- **pH:** 6.0-6.5
- **Temperature:** Water 16-20°C, Air 15-21°C (cool season)
- **Light:** Medium (DLI 12-16, PPFD 250-400)
- **Water:** 2.0-3.0 L/day per plant (high water needs!)

### Fertilizer Schedule (NPK)
- **Germination:** 5-5-5 (weekly, very light)
- **Vegetative (Early):** 10-5-7 (weekly)
- **Vegetative (Mid):** 15-5-10 (weekly) - HIGH nitrogen for stalk growth
- **Maturation:** 10-10-15 (weekly) - balanced for quality

### Pests (UC Davis IPM)
1. **Celery Aphid** (Cavariella aegopodii)
   - Severity: Medium-High
   - Organic: Insecticidal soap, neem oil (difficult to reach in tight stalks!)
   - Biological: Aphidius wasps, ladybugs
   - Cultural: Yellow sticky traps, remove infested plants early

2. **Celery Leafminer** (Liriomyza trifolii)
   - Severity: High (disfigures leaves/stalks)
   - Organic: Spinosad, remove mined leaves
   - Biological: Parasitic wasps (Diglyphus isaea)
   - Cultural: Yellow sticky traps, destroy infested leaves

3. **Cutworms**
   - Severity: Medium (young plants)
   - Organic: Bt, hand-pick at night
   - Biological: Beneficial nematodes
   - Cultural: Collars around seedlings

### Diseases (UC Davis IPM)
1. **Late Blight** (Septoria apiicola)
   - Severity: High
   - Symptoms: Small brown spots with yellow halos on leaves
   - Organic: Copper fungicide, remove infected plants
   - Prevention: Avoid overhead watering, good air circulation, certified seed

2. **Early Blight** (Cercospora apii)
   - Severity: High
   - Symptoms: Circular tan spots on older leaves
   - Organic: Copper fungicide
   - Prevention: Crop rotation, avoid wetting foliage

3. **Pink Rot** (Sclerotinia sclerotiorum)
   - Severity: Medium
   - Symptoms: Water-soaked lesions at soil line, pink mycelium
   - Organic: Remove infected plants, improve drainage
   - Prevention: Avoid overwatering, good air circulation

4. **Black Heart** (Calcium deficiency)
   - Severity: Medium (physiological disorder)
   - Symptoms: Black dead tissue in center of plant
   - Organic: Foliar calcium spray, calcium nitrate
   - Prevention: Consistent watering, adequate calcium in nutrient solution

### Environmental Requirements
- **Temperature:** Min 10°C, Max 24°C, Optimal 18°C
- **Humidity:** 60-80%, Optimal 70% (likes higher humidity than most crops!)
- **Light Hours:** 14-16 hours
- **CO2:** 800-1000 ppm
- **Soil pH:** 6.0-7.0

### UAE Adaptations
- **Heat Tolerance:** VERY LOW - Cannot tolerate UAE heat, bolts >24°C
- **Cold Tolerance:** MEDIUM - Tolerates light frost but damaged <-2°C
- **Water Requirement:** VERY HIGH (2-3 L/day, sensitive to water stress)
- **Greenhouse Required:** Yes (climate control essential)
- **Summer Outdoor:** NOT suitable (bolts immediately in heat)
- **Winter Outdoor:** POSSIBLE but challenging (Dec-Feb only, narrow window)
- **Best Planting Months:** November, December, January
- **Local Challenges:** VERY LONG growth cycle (120+ days) difficult in UAE short cool season; extremely sensitive to water stress (causes stringy stalks, black heart); bolts quickly when temps rise; requires consistent cool temps
- **Local Recommendations:** DIFFICULT crop for UAE! Only attempt in climate-controlled greenhouse with 16-20°C year-round; choose faster varieties (100-110 days); very high water needs; consider importing rather than growing commercially; blanch stalks (exclude light) for white/tender celery; high value but challenging

### Companion Plants
- **Good:** Cabbage, Cauliflower, Leek, Tomato, Bush Beans
- **Bad:** Corn, Aster flowers (attracts aster leafhopper)
- **Reasoning:** Benefits from brassicas; avoid crops that attract similar pests

### Economics
- **Market Price:** 15-20 AED/kg
- **Demand:** HIGH (popular in Middle Eastern, Western cuisine)
- **Shelf Life:** 14-21 days (refrigerated 0-2°C, very high humidity 95-98%, plastic bag)
- **Storage:** Good shelf life if kept humid and cold

### Quality Grading
- **Premium:** Crisp thick stalks, light green color, tight bunch, no stringiness, no black heart, 30-40 cm tall
- **Standard:** Reasonably crisp, some stringiness ok, minor blemishes
- **Reject:** Limp/wilted stalks, severe stringiness, black heart, disease, brown discoloration

### Sources

- Cornell Vegetable Program: Celery Production
- NC State Extension: Celery Culture

### Botanical Information

- **Type:** Annual, warm-season grass
- **Common Names:** Sweet corn, Sugar corn
- **Growth Habit:** Tall upright monoecious grass (separate male/female flowers)

### Growth Cycle

- **Total Cycle:** 65-90 days (varies by variety: early, mid, late season)

### Yield
- **Yield per Plant:** 1-2 ears per plant, 0.3-0.5 kg total per plant
- **Waste:** 60% (husk, cob, silk)

### Hydroponic Requirements
- **EC:** 1.8-2.4 mS/cm (1260-1680 ppm)
- **pH:** 6.0-7.0
- **Temperature:** Water 18-24°C, Air 21-30°C
- **Light:** VERY HIGH (DLI 20-25, PPFD 600-800) - full sun crop!
- **Water:** 2.0-4.0 L/day per plant (especially during silking/ear fill)

**Note:** Sweet corn is RARELY grown hydroponically due to:
- Large size (2-3 m tall)
- High light requirements
- Wind pollination needs (must grow in blocks, not single plants)
- Low value per plant compared to space/resources
- Short harvest window

### Fertilizer Schedule (NPK)
- **Germination:** 5-5-5 (weekly)
- **Vegetative (Early):** 15-5-10 (weekly) - HIGH nitrogen for rapid growth
- **Vegetative (Late):** 20-10-10 (weekly) - maximum nitrogen before tasseling
- **Silking/Pollination:** 10-20-20 (weekly) - reduce N, boost P&K
- **Ear Fill:** 5-20-25 (weekly) - high K for sugar development

### Pests (UC Davis IPM)
1. **Corn Earworm** (Helicoverpa zea)
   - Severity: VERY HIGH (major pest!)
   - Organic: Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis), Spinosad, mineral oil on silk
   - Biological: Trichogramma wasps, predatory beetles
   - Cultural: Hand-pick, destroy infested ears, pheromone traps

2. **European Corn Borer** (Ostrinia nubilalis)
   - Severity: High
   - Organic: Bt, destroy stalks after harvest
   - Biological: Trichogramma wasps
   - Cultural: Crop rotation, destroy crop residue

3. **Aphids** (Corn leaf aphid)
   - Severity: Medium
   - Organic: Insecticidal soap, neem oil, strong water spray
   - Biological: Ladybugs, lacewings
   - Cultural: Monitor tassels and whorls

4. **Cutworms**
   - Severity: Medium (young plants)
   - Organic: Bt, hand-pick at night
   - Biological: Beneficial nematodes
   - Cultural: Collars around seedlings, remove debris

### Diseases (UC Davis IPM)
1. **Common Rust** (Puccinia sorghi)
   - Severity: Medium-High
   - Symptoms: Orange-brown pustules on leaves
   - Organic: Sulfur spray (limited effectiveness)
   - Prevention: Resistant varieties, good air circulation

2. **Gray Leaf Spot** (Cercospora zeae-maydis)
   - Severity: Medium
   - Symptoms: Rectangular gray-tan lesions on leaves
   - Organic: Remove infected plants
   - Prevention: Crop rotation, destroy debris, resistant varieties

3. **Northern Corn Leaf Blight**
   - Severity: Medium
   - Symptoms: Long elliptical tan lesions on leaves
   - Organic: None effective
   - Prevention: Resistant varieties, crop rotation

4. **Smut** (Ustilago maydis)
   - Severity: Low-Medium
   - Symptoms: Large gray-white galls on ears, tassels
   - Organic: Remove and destroy galls before they open
   - Prevention: Avoid mechanical damage, remove galls promptly

### Environmental Requirements
- **Temperature:** Min 15°C, Max 35°C, Optimal 25°C
- **Humidity:** 50-70%, Optimal 60%
- **Light Hours:** 12-14 hours (full sun!)
- **CO2:** 800-1000 ppm
- **Soil pH:** 6.0-7.0

### UAE Adaptations
- **Heat Tolerance:** HIGH - Tolerates warm temperatures 25-32°C well
- **Cold Tolerance:** VERY LOW - Frost-sensitive, damaged <10°C
- **Water Requirement:** VERY HIGH (3-4 L/day during silking/ear fill)
- **Greenhouse Required:** No (field crop, requires open pollination)
- **Summer Outdoor:** CHALLENGING (can tolerate heat but silking may fail >38°C)
- **Winter Outdoor:** EXCELLENT (Feb-Apr and Oct-Nov)
- **Best Planting Months:** February, March, October, November
- **Local Challenges:** High water needs in desert; wind pollination requires blocks of minimum 16 plants (4x4); corn earworm severe in UAE climate; must harvest at exact peak sweetness (sugars convert to starch rapidly after picking)
- **Local Recommendations:** NOT recommended for hydroponics! Grow as open-field crop Feb-Apr and Oct-Nov; plant in blocks for pollination; choose fast-maturing varieties (70-80 days); very high water needs; moderate demand in UAE market; consider as rotation crop for farms

### Companion Plants
- **Good:** Beans (three sisters), Squash, Cucumber, Peas
- **Bad:** Tomato, Celery
- **Reasoning:** Three sisters system: corn provides trellis for beans, beans fix nitrogen for corn, squash shades soil; tomato attracts earworms

### Economics
- **Market Price:** 8-12 AED/kg (whole ears), 2-4 AED per ear
- **Demand:** MEDIUM (popular but seasonal)
- **Shelf Life:** 3-7 days (refrigerated 0°C, very high humidity 95-98%)
- **Storage:** VERY SHORT shelf life! Sugars convert to starch rapidly; eat within 24 hours for best quality

### Quality Grading
- **Premium:** Full plump kernels, milky juice when pierced, tight green husk, moist silk, ear 18-20 cm, no worm damage
- **Standard:** Reasonably full kernels, some tip unfilled ok, minor blemishes
- **Reject:** Worm damage, dry kernels, incomplete pollination, disease

### Sources
- UC Davis IPM: Sweet Corn Pest Management
- NC State Extension: Sweet Corn Production
- University of Minnesota Extension: Growing Sweet Corn
- USDA PLANTS: Zea mays verification

## Plant 37: Drumstick / Moringa

**Scientific Name:** Moringa oleifera Lam.

### Botanical Information
- **Family:** Moringaceae (Moringa family)
- **Type:** Perennial tree (grown as annual for pods in temperate climates)
- **Common Names:** Moringa, Drumstick tree, Horseradish tree, Sahjan (Hindi), Murungai (Tamil)
- **Growth Habit:** Fast-growing tree, 3-12 m tall
- **USDA Verification:** Moringa oleifera Lam.
- **Special Note:** Leaves also edible and highly nutritious ("superfood")

### Growth Cycle (for pod production, annual culture)
- **Germination:** 7-14 days
- **Vegetative Stage:** 90-120 days (until flowering)
- **Flowering Stage:** 30-45 days
- **Fruiting/Pod Development:** 60-90 days (from flowering to mature pod)
- **Harvest Duration:** 60-90 days (multiple harvests)
- **Total Cycle:** 180-270 days (6-9 months to first pods)
- **Perennial Production:** Year 2+ produces 2-3 crops per year

### Yield
- **Yield per Plant:** 50-100 pods per tree annually (young annual), 200-400 pods (mature perennial)
- **Pod Weight:** 0.3-0.5 kg per 10 pods
- **Total Yield:** 6-20 kg per plant per year (varies with age/management)
- **Waste:** 40% (seeds removed, only pod shell eaten)

### Hydroponic Requirements
**Note:** Moringa is RARELY grown hydroponically due to large tree size. Most suitable for open field or large containers.

- **EC:** 2.0-3.0 mS/cm (if grown in pots/hydro)
- **pH:** 6.0-7.0 (tolerates 5.0-9.0, very adaptable!)
- **Temperature:** Water 20-30°C, Air 25-35°C
- **Light:** VERY HIGH (full sun required, DLI 20-25, PPFD 600-800)
- **Water:** 2.0-5.0 L/day (mature tree, high in summer)

### Fertilizer Schedule (NPK)
- **Germination:** 5-5-5 (weekly)
- **Vegetative:** 15-10-10 (bi-weekly) - high N for rapid growth
- **Pre-Flowering:** 10-20-15 (weekly) - boost P for flower buds
- **Flowering/Podding:** 5-20-20 (weekly) - high P&K for pod development

### Pests
1. **Pod Fly** (Gitona sp.)
   - Severity: High (major pest on pods!)
   - Organic: Kaolin clay, neem oil, remove infested pods
   - Biological: Parasitic wasps
   - Cultural: Bag pods, remove fallen pods promptly

2. **Leaf Caterpillars** (various)
   - Severity: Medium
   - Organic: Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis), hand-pick
   - Biological: Birds, parasitic wasps
   - Cultural: Monitor regularly

3. **Aphids**
   - Severity: Low
   - Organic: Insecticidal soap, neem oil
   - Biological: Ladybugs
   - Cultural: Strong water spray

4. **Termites** (on woody stems)
   - Severity: Low-Medium
   - Organic: Neem oil around base
   - Biological: None specific
   - Cultural: Avoid wood mulch near trunk

### Diseases
1. **Root Rot** (Pythium, Rhizoctonia)
   - Severity: Medium (in poorly drained soil)
   - Symptoms: Wilting, yellowing, brown mushy roots
   - Organic: Improve drainage, biological fungicides
   - Prevention: Well-drained soil, avoid overwatering

2. **Leaf Spot** (Cercospora sp.)
   - Severity: Low
   - Symptoms: Brown spots on leaves
   - Organic: Copper fungicide, remove infected leaves
   - Prevention: Good air circulation

3. **Powdery Mildew**
   - Severity: Low
   - Symptoms: White powdery coating on leaves
   - Organic: Sulfur spray, milk spray
   - Prevention: Good air circulation, avoid overhead watering

### Environmental Requirements
- **Temperature:** Min 10°C, Max 48°C, Optimal 28°C (VERY heat-tolerant!)
- **Humidity:** 20-80%, Optimal 50% (drought-tolerant once established)
- **Light Hours:** 12-14 hours (full sun)
- **CO2:** 800-1000 ppm
- **Soil pH:** 6.0-7.0 (tolerates 5.0-9.0)

### UAE Adaptations
- **Heat Tolerance:** VERY HIGH - THRIVES in extreme UAE heat! Tolerates up to 48°C!
- **Cold Tolerance:** LOW - Sensitive to frost, defoliates <10°C
- **Water Requirement:** LOW-MEDIUM once established (drought-tolerant!)
- **Greenhouse Required:** No (LOVES outdoor UAE climate!)
- **Summer Outdoor:** EXCELLENT (May-Oct, peak growth in hottest months!)
- **Winter Outdoor:** GOOD (Nov-Apr, slows but continues growing)
- **Best Planting Months:** March, April (establish before summer)
- **Local Challenges:** Slow to first pod production (6-9 months); large tree size requires space; can be invasive if not managed
- **Local Recommendations:** PERFECT for UAE! Heat-loving perennial tree; extremely drought-tolerant once established; pods highly popular in South Asian cuisine (massive expat market in UAE); leaves as nutritious "superfood" vegetable; plant as border/hedge for farms; fast growth (3-4 m in first year); produces year-round in UAE; low water/maintenance needs; excellent permaculture crop for desert

### Companion Plants
- **Good:** Most crops (light shade tolerant), Nitrogen-fixing cover crops
- **Bad:** None specific (but provides too much shade for sun-loving crops if close)
- **Reasoning:** Can provide light shade and windbreak for other crops; deep roots don't compete

### Economics
- **Market Price:** 15-25 AED/kg (fresh pods), 50-100 AED/kg (dried leaf powder)
- **Demand:** VERY HIGH in South Asian communities (Indian, Pakistani, Bangladeshi expats)
- **Shelf Life:** 7-10 days (refrigerated 7-10°C, high humidity)
- **Storage:** Fresh pods refrigerated; leaves dried and powdered for long-term storage (6-12 months)

### Quality Grading (Pods)
- **Premium:** Young tender pods 20-40 cm, pencil-thin, bright green, no seeds developed, snap easily
- **Standard:** Pods 40-60 cm, slightly thicker, minor blemishes ok
- **Reject:** Tough fibrous pods, mature hard seeds, yellowing, >60 cm (too tough)

### Sources
- FAO: Moringa Cultivation and Use
- USDA PLANTS: Moringa oleifera verification
- South Asian agricultural research: Pod production systems
- Trees for Life: Moringa cultivation guide

## Plant 38: Fennel (Florence Fennel, Bulb Fennel)

**Scientific Name:** Foeniculum vulgare var. azoricum (Mill.) Thell.

### Botanical Information
- **Family:** Apiaceae (Carrot family)
- **Type:** Biennial grown as annual
- **Common Names:** Florence fennel, Finocchio, Bulb fennel
- **Growth Habit:** Rosette forming swollen bulb-like base
- **USDA Verification:** Foeniculum vulgare var. azoricum
- **Note:** Different from herb fennel (Foeniculum vulgare) grown for leaves/seeds

### Growth Cycle
- **Germination:** 7-14 days
- **Vegetative Stage:** 60-90 days (bulb formation)
- **Flowering Stage:** 0 (harvested before bolting)
- **Fruiting Stage:** 0
- **Harvest Duration:** 7-14 days
- **Total Cycle:** 70-100 days

### Yield
- **Yield per Plant:** 0.3-0.8 kg per bulb
- **Waste:** 30% (outer layers, stalks, fronds)

### Hydroponic Requirements
- **EC:** 1.0-1.4 mS/cm (700-980 ppm) - LOWER than most crops!
- **pH:** 6.4-6.8
- **Temperature:** Water 15-18°C, Air 15-20°C (cool season, very sensitive!)
- **Light:** Medium (DLI 12-15, PPFD 250-350)
- **Water:** 1.5-2.0 L/day per plant

### Fertilizer Schedule (NPK)
- **Germination:** 5-5-5 (weekly)
- **Vegetative:** 10-5-10 (weekly) - balanced for bulb formation
- **Bulb Swelling:** 5-10-15 (weekly) - reduce N, increase K for bulb quality
- **Final Stage:** 3-10-15 (bi-weekly) - low N prevents bolting

### Pests (UC Davis IPM)
1. **Aphids**
   - Severity: Medium
   - Organic: Insecticidal soap, neem oil
   - Biological: Ladybugs, Aphidius wasps
   - Cultural: Monitor new growth

2. **Cutworms**
   - Severity: Medium (young plants)
   - Organic: Bt, hand-pick at night
   - Biological: Beneficial nematodes
   - Cultural: Collars around seedlings

3. **Slugs & Snails**
   - Severity: Low
   - Organic: Iron phosphate bait, beer traps
   - Biological: None specific
   - Cultural: Remove hiding places, hand-pick

### Diseases
1. **Fusarium Wilt** (Fusarium oxysporum)
   - Severity: Medium
   - Symptoms: Yellowing, wilting, brown vascular tissue
   - Organic: Remove infected plants, long rotation
   - Prevention: Use disease-free transplants, avoid contaminated soil

2. **Pythium Root Rot**
   - Severity: Medium
   - Symptoms: Brown mushy roots, wilting
   - Organic: Biological fungicides
   - Prevention: Well-drained media, avoid overwatering

3. **Downy Mildew**
   - Severity: Low
   - Symptoms: Yellow patches on leaves, white mold
   - Organic: Copper fungicide
   - Prevention: Good air circulation, reduce humidity

### Environmental Requirements
- **Temperature:** Min 5°C, Max 25°C, Optimal 18°C (VERY temperature-sensitive!)
- **Humidity:** 50-70%, Optimal 60%
- **Light Hours:** 12-14 hours
- **CO2:** 800-1000 ppm
- **Soil pH:** 6.0-7.0

### UAE Adaptations
- **Heat Tolerance:** VERY LOW - Bolts immediately in heat >25°C
- **Cold Tolerance:** HIGH - Tolerates light frost
- **Water Requirement:** MEDIUM (1.5-2.0 L/day)
- **Greenhouse Required:** Yes (precise temperature control essential!)
- **Summer Outdoor:** NOT suitable (bolts instantly in heat)
- **Winter Outdoor:** CHALLENGING (Nov-Jan only, narrow window, temperature swings cause bolting)
- **Best Planting Months:** November, December, January
- **Local Challenges:** EXTREMELY sensitive to temperature stress - ANY temperature swing >25°C or sudden cold causes immediate bolting (goes to flower without forming bulb); requires consistent cool 15-20°C for 80-90 days
- **Local Recommendations:** VERY DIFFICULT crop for UAE! Only attempt in climate-controlled greenhouse with precise 15-20°C; choose bolt-resistant varieties; direct seed (transplanting can trigger bolting); high-value Mediterranean vegetable but extremely challenging; consider importing rather than growing commercially

### Companion Plants
- **Good:** Most crops (allelopathic to few plants as culinary fennel)
- **Bad:** Tomato, Bean, Kohlrabi, Caraway
- **Reasoning:** Fennel secretes substance that inhibits some plants; keep isolated

### Economics
- **Market Price:** 20-30 AED/kg
- **Demand:** MEDIUM (popular in Mediterranean, European cuisine)
- **Shelf Life:** 10-14 days (refrigerated 0-2°C, high humidity 95%)
- **Storage:** Good shelf life if kept cold and humid

### Quality Grading
- **Premium:** Firm white bulbs, 8-12 cm diameter, no bolting, crisp texture, mild anise flavor
- **Standard:** Reasonably firm, slight discoloration ok
- **Reject:** Bolting (flower stalk), split bulbs, brown discoloration, limp texture

### Sources
- UC Davis: Fennel Production
- Johnny's Seeds: Florence Fennel Culture
- Cornell Vegetable Program: Specialty Vegetables
- Hydroponic research: EC/pH requirements

## Plant 39: Onion - Red (Red Bulb Onion)

**Scientific Name:** Allium cepa L.

### Botanical Information
- **Family:** Amaryllidaceae (formerly Alliaceae, Onion family)
- **Type:** Biennial grown as annual
- **Common Names:** Red onion, Purple onion
- **Growth Habit:** Bulb with upright hollow leaves
- **USDA Verification:** Allium cepa L.
- **Photoperiod:** Day-length sensitive (long-day, intermediate-day, or short-day varieties)

### Growth Cycle
- **Germination:** 7-10 days (from seed), 0 days (from sets)
- **Vegetative Stage:** 90-120 days (leaf production)
- **Bulbing Stage:** 30-60 days (bulb swelling, triggered by day length)
- **Curing Stage:** 14-21 days (after harvest, drying)
- **Flowering Stage:** 0 (harvested at bulb stage)
- **Fruiting Stage:** 0
- **Total Cycle:** 120-180 days from seed, 90-120 days from sets

**Day-Length Requirements:**
- Short-day: <12 hours (for low latitudes, UAE suitable!)
- Intermediate-day: 12-14 hours
- Long-day: >14 hours (for high latitudes)

### Yield
- **Yield per Plant:** 0.15-0.35 kg per bulb (varies with variety)
- **Waste:** 10-15% (outer dry skins, roots, tops)

### Hydroponic Requirements
**Note:** Onions are RARELY grown hydroponically due to:
- Very long growing season (120-180 days)
- Low value per plant
- Photoperiod requirements (need correct day-length variety)
- Better suited for field production

- **EC:** 1.4-1.8 mS/cm (980-1260 ppm) - LOWER than most crops
- **pH:** 6.0-6.7
- **Temperature:** Water 15-20°C, Air 13-24°C
- **Light:** Medium-High (DLI 14-18, PPFD 350-450)
- **Water:** 0.5-1.5 L/day (depends on bulb size)

### Fertilizer Schedule (NPK)
- **Germination:** 5-5-5 (weekly)
- **Vegetative (Leaf Growth):** 15-5-10 (weekly) - HIGH nitrogen for leaf production
- **Bulbing (Early):** 10-10-15 (weekly) - balanced
- **Bulbing (Late):** 5-10-20 (weekly) - reduce N, high K for bulb quality
- **Pre-Harvest:** 0-0-0 (stop fertilizing 3-4 weeks before harvest)

### Pests (UC Davis IPM)
1. **Onion Thrips** (Thrips tabaci)
   - Severity: VERY HIGH (major pest!)
   - Organic: Spinosad, insecticidal soap (difficult to control!)
   - Biological: Predatory mites, minute pirate bugs
   - Cultural: Blue sticky traps, reflective mulch, destroy crop debris

2. **Onion Maggot** (Delia antiqua)
   - Severity: High
   - Organic: Beneficial nematodes
   - Biological: Ground beetles, rove beetles
   - Cultural: Row covers, avoid planting near previous onion crops

3. **Cutworms**
   - Severity: Medium (young plants)
   - Organic: Bt, hand-pick at night
   - Biological: Beneficial nematodes
   - Cultural: Collars around seedlings

### Diseases (UC Davis IPM)
1. **Purple Blotch** (Alternaria porri)
   - Severity: High
   - Symptoms: White lesions with purple borders on leaves
   - Organic: Copper fungicide
   - Prevention: Avoid overhead watering, good air circulation, crop rotation

2. **Downy Mildew** (Peronospora destructor)
   - Severity: High
   - Symptoms: Pale green patches on leaves, gray-purple fuzzy growth
   - Organic: Copper fungicide
   - Prevention: Good air circulation, avoid overhead watering, resistant varieties

3. **Botrytis Leaf Blight** (Botrytis squamosa)
   - Severity: High
   - Symptoms: Small white flecks on leaves turning tan
   - Organic: Copper fungicide, remove infected plants
   - Prevention: Good air circulation, avoid wetting foliage

4. **Pink Root** (Phoma terrestris)
   - Severity: Medium
   - Symptoms: Roots turn pink then purple then die
   - Organic: No cure, remove infected plants
   - Prevention: Use resistant varieties, long crop rotation, avoid contaminated soil

### Environmental Requirements
- **Temperature:** Min 7°C, Max 30°C, Optimal 20°C
- **Humidity:** 50-70%, Optimal 60%
- **Light Hours:** Depends on variety (short-day <12h, intermediate 12-14h, long-day >14h)
- **CO2:** 800-1000 ppm
- **Soil pH:** 6.0-7.0

### UAE Adaptations
- **Heat Tolerance:** MEDIUM - Tolerates warm temps but bulbing stops >30°C
- **Cold Tolerance:** HIGH - Tolerates light frost, improved bulb quality
- **Water Requirement:** MEDIUM (1-1.5 L/day, reduce before harvest)
- **Greenhouse Required:** No (field crop)
- **Summer Outdoor:** NOT suitable (too hot for bulbing)
- **Winter Outdoor:** EXCELLENT (Oct-Mar)
- **Best Planting Months:** October, November, December (SHORT-DAY varieties ONLY for UAE latitude!)
- **Local Challenges:** MUST use short-day varieties (long-day won't bulb in UAE low latitude!); long growing season (120+ days); onion thrips very difficult to control; requires dry period before harvest for curing
- **Local Recommendations:** Grow Oct-Mar using SHORT-DAY or INTERMEDIATE-DAY varieties only; use onion sets instead of seed for faster maturity (90 days vs 150 days); popular vegetable with steady demand; good storage crop; red onions have higher value than yellow; stop irrigation 2-3 weeks before harvest and cure in dry shade for 2-3 weeks for best storage

### Companion Plants
- **Good:** Carrot, Beet, Lettuce, Cabbage, Tomato
- **Bad:** Bean, Pea, Asparagus, Sage
- **Reasoning:** Alliums repel many pests; avoid legumes (onions stunt their growth)

### Economics
- **Market Price:** 4-8 AED/kg (red onions typically higher than yellow)
- **Demand:** VERY HIGH (essential vegetable, steady demand)
- **Shelf Life:** 60-120 days (cured, stored in cool dry place 10-15°C, low humidity)
- **Storage:** Excellent storage crop if properly cured

### Quality Grading
- **Premium:** Firm hard bulbs, deep red-purple color, dry papery skins, 6-10 cm diameter, no sprouting, no disease
- **Standard:** Reasonably firm, minor blemishes ok, some green tops ok
- **Reject:** Soft bulbs, sprouting, disease, split bulbs, poor color

- NC State Extension: Onion Production
