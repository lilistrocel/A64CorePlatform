## 1. WATERMELON (Citrullus lanatus)

### Basic Information
- **Scientific Name:** Citrullus lanatus (Thunb.) Matsum. & Nakai
- **Common Names:** Watermelon
- **Family:** Cucurbitaceae
- **Farm Type Compatibility:** open_field, greenhouse, hydroponic

### Growth Cycle
- **Germination:** 4-12 days (75-95°F/24-35°C optimal)
- **Transplant Age:** 3-4 weeks
- **Days to Harvest:** 80-100 days from seeding (varies by variety)
- **Total Cycle:** 90-110 days
- **Flowering to Harvest:** 35-40 days

### Yield Information
- **Yield Per Plant:** 15-25 kg (2-4 fruits per vine depending on variety)
- **Expected Waste:** 45% (rind not edible)

### Fertilizer Schedule
**Soil-Based:**
- Pre-plant: 80-100 kg/ha N, 25-60 kg/ha P, 35-80 kg/ha K
- Side-dress: Apply when vines begin to run and again at flowering

**NPK Recommendations:**
- Early growth: 5-10-10 (balanced with higher P-K)
- Fruiting: Increase potassium for sugar content

**Hydroponic:**
- NPK 18-18-18+TE at 500 ppm
- Change solution every 2-3 weeks

### Hydroponic Parameters
- **pH:** 5.8-6.4 (soil: 6.0-6.8)
- **EC:** 1.2-1.5 mS/cm (DWC/RDWC systems)
- **Temperature:** 75-85°F (24-29°C) optimal, prefers 22-30°C mean daily
- **Humidity:** 50-70%
- **Light:** Full sun, 8-10 hours minimum
- **Spacing:** 3-6 feet (90-180 cm) between plants in rows 6-8 feet apart
- **Salinity Tolerance:** Moderately sensitive (ECe threshold 2.5 mmhos/cm)

### Common Pests & Diseases
1. **Cucumber Beetles** (HIGH severity)
   - Symptoms: Leaf damage, transmit bacterial wilt
   - Prevention: Row covers, reflective mulches, crop rotation
   - Treatment: Pyrethrin, neem oil (evening application)

2. **Aphids** (MEDIUM severity)
   - Symptoms: Curled leaves, honeydew, virus vectors
   - Prevention: Beneficial insects, reflective mulches
   - Treatment: Insecticidal soap, neem oil

3. **Anthracnose** (HIGH severity)
   - Caused by Colletotrichum species
   - Symptoms: Sunken lesions on fruit, leaf spots
   - Prevention: Crop rotation, resistant varieties, avoid overhead watering
   - Treatment: Fungicides (chlorothalonil, copper-based)

4. **Fusarium Wilt** (HIGH severity)
   - Symptoms: Wilting vines, yellowing, vascular discoloration
   - Prevention: Resistant varieties, crop rotation, soil solarization
   - Treatment: No cure - prevention critical

5. **Powdery Mildew** (MEDIUM-HIGH severity)
   - Symptoms: White powdery coating on leaves
   - Prevention: Good air circulation, resistant varieties
   - Treatment: Sulfur fungicides, potassium bicarbonate

### IPM Strategies
- Use row covers early season to exclude cucumber beetles
- 3-4 year crop rotation with non-cucurbits
- Plant resistant varieties when available
- Remove and destroy crop residue after harvest
- Water at base of plants, avoid wetting foliage
- Monitor regularly, treat early

### Companion Planting
- **Beneficial:** Radish, Corn, Beans, Marigolds, Nasturtiums
- **Incompatible:** Potatoes, other cucurbits (cross-pollination, disease sharing)

### UAE Adaptations
- **Season:** Hot season - March to July (loves heat!)
- **Planting Months:** March, April, May
- **Harvest Months:** June, July, August, September
- **Heat Tolerance:** VERY HIGH (native to tropical/subtropical Africa, thrives in desert with water)
- **Water Requirement:** HIGH (critical during fruit development)
- **Greenhouse:** Not needed - prefers outdoor heat
- **Notes:** "EXCELLENT for UAE! Native to dry tropical Africa, watermelon thrives in hot, dry desert climate (22-30°C optimal). Fruits grown in hot, dry UAE conditions have higher sugar content (11% vs 8% in humid climates). Requires abundant groundwater/irrigation. Very popular in UAE markets. Can tolerate moderate salinity. Plant in spring for summer harvest. High market demand, premium prices for quality fruit."

### Economics
- **Market Price:** AED 3-6/kg (varies by variety and season)
- **Labor Hours:** 0.3 hours/plant (low maintenance once established)

### Quality Grading
- **Grade A:** Uniform shape, symmetrical, firm, heavy for size, yellow ground spot, hollow sound when tapped, 5-10 kg
- **Grade B:** Slight irregularities, 3-5 kg or >10 kg, minor surface marks OK

## 2. ROCK MELON / CANTALOUPE (Cucumis melo var. cantalupensis)

### Basic Information
- **Scientific Name:** Cucumis melo L. var. cantalupensis
- **Common Names:** Cantaloupe, Rock Melon, Muskmelon (netted varieties)
- **Family:** Cucurbitaceae
- **Farm Type Compatibility:** open_field, greenhouse, hydroponic

### Growth Cycle
- **Germination:** 4-8 days (70-90°F/21-32°C)
- **Transplant Age:** 3-4 weeks
- **Days to Harvest:** 70-90 days from seeding
- **Total Cycle:** 80-100 days
- **Flowering to Harvest:** 35-45 days

### Yield Information
- **Yield Per Plant:** 8-12 kg (2-4 fruits per plant)
- **Expected Waste:** 40% (rind, seeds)

### Fertilizer Schedule
**Pre-plant:**
- Apply balanced fertilizer based on soil test

**After plants reach 4+ inches:**
- Balanced fertilizer (10-10-10)

**After flowering:**
- Switch to higher P-K formula (5-10-10) for fruit development
- Side-dress at vine running and fruit set

### Hydroponic Parameters
- **pH:** 5.8-6.4 (soil: 6.0-6.8)
- **EC:** 1.5-2.0 mS/cm
- **Temperature:** 70-85°F (21-29°C) optimal, prefers 18-30°C
- **Humidity:** 50-70% (lower during ripening to increase sugar)
- **Light:** Full sun, minimum 8 hours
- **Spacing:** 2-3 feet (60-90 cm) apart, rows 4-6 feet

### Common Pests & Diseases
1. **Aphids** (HIGH severity - virus vectors)
   - Transmit Cucumber Mosaic Virus, other viral diseases
   - Prevention: Row covers, reflective mulches, virus-free transplants
   - Treatment: Insecticidal soap, systemic insecticides for severe infestations

2. **Cucumber Beetles** (HIGH severity)
   - Major carriers of bacterial wilt
   - Prevention: Row covers (remove for pollination), trap crops
   - Treatment: Pyrethrins, neem oil

3. **Spider Mites** (MEDIUM severity)
   - Symptoms: Stippling, bronzing, webbing on leaves
   - Prevention: Maintain humidity, avoid drought stress
   - Treatment: Miticides, predatory mites

4. **Fungal Diseases** (HIGH severity)
   - Anthracnose, Downy Mildew, Powdery Mildew, Fusarium Wilt
   - Prevention: Resistant varieties, crop rotation (3-4 years), drip irrigation
   - Treatment: Fungicides (copper-based, chlorothalonil), remove infected plants

5. **Viral Diseases** (HIGH severity)
   - CMV, CYSDV, WMV, SqMV, ZYMV (all transmitted by aphids/beetles)
   - Prevention: Control vectors, use resistant varieties, remove infected plants immediately
   - Treatment: No cure - prevention critical

### IPM Strategies
- 3-4 year crop rotation
- Use disease-resistant varieties
- Row covers early season
- Water at base in early morning
- Mulch to reduce weeds and soil splash
- Remove and destroy infected plants

### Companion Planting
- **Beneficial:** Corn, Radish, Beans, Marigolds, Nasturtiums, Sunflowers
- **Incompatible:** Potatoes, other melons (disease cross-contamination)

### UAE Adaptations
- **Season:** Warm season - March to June
- **Planting Months:** March, April, May
- **Harvest Months:** May, June, July, August
- **Heat Tolerance:** HIGH (loves heat 18-30°C, can tolerate up to 35°C)
- **Water Requirement:** HIGH (consistent moisture for fruit development)
- **Greenhouse:** Optional (can extend season, improve quality)
- **Notes:** "GOOD for UAE spring/summer production! Heat-loving crop that thrives in desert conditions with irrigation. Popular in local and export markets. Sweet, aromatic fruit. Greenhouse production can extend season and improve quality control. Resistant varieties available for common diseases. Well-suited for drip irrigation systems."

### Economics
- **Market Price:** AED 6-10/kg
- **Labor Hours:** 0.4 hours/plant

### Quality Grading
- **Grade A:** Full slip (separates from stem cleanly), strong aroma, firm flesh, good netting, 1-2 kg
- **Grade B:** Half-slip acceptable, minor netting irregularities, 0.8-1 kg or >2 kg

## 6. ASH GOURD / WINTER MELON (Benincasa hispida)

- **Scientific Name:** Benincasa hispida (Thunb.) Cogn.
- **Common Names:** Ash Gourd, Winter Melon, Wax Gourd, White Pumpkin, Chinese Watermelon
- **Family:** Cucurbitaceae
- **Farm Type Compatibility:** open_field, greenhouse, hydroponic

- **Germination:** 7-14 days (75-85°F/24-29°C)
- **Days to Harvest:**
  - Immature (green): 45-60 days
  - Mature (full-size): 100-120 days
- **Total Cycle:** 110-130 days for mature fruit
- **Flowering to Harvest:** 50-70 days

### Yield Information
- **Yield Per Plant:** 20-40 kg (1-3 large fruits, 5-20 kg each)
- **Expected Waste:** 30% (rind, seeds)

**Pre-plant:**
- Rich, fertile soil with organic matter
- 10-10-10 all-purpose fertilizer

**Growing Season:**
- Fertilize every 2-3 weeks during growing season
- Side-dress with compost when fruiting begins
- Higher K and P fertilizer to encourage flowering/fruiting (5-10-10)

### Hydroponic Parameters
- **pH:** 6.0-6.8 (soil and hydroponic)
- **EC:** 1.8-2.5 mS/cm
- **Temperature:** 75-90°F (24-32°C) optimal - loves heat!
- **Humidity:** 60-80% (tropical preference)
- **Light:** Full sun, 8-10 hours
- **Spacing:** 4-6 feet (120-180 cm) apart - large vining plant
- **Support:** Requires strong trellis for large fruits

### Common Pests & Diseases
**Similar to other cucurbits:**
1. **Fruit Fly** (HIGH severity)

3. **Spider Mites** (MEDIUM severity)
4. **Powdery Mildew** (MEDIUM-HIGH severity)

### UAE Adaptations

- **Planting Months:** April, May, June
- **Harvest Months:** July, August, September, October (mature fruits), June-August (immature)
- **Heat Tolerance:** VERY HIGH (tropical origin, loves heat 24-32°C)
- **Water Requirement:** HIGH (consistent moisture critical)
- **Greenhouse:** Not needed - thrives in outdoor heat
- **Notes:** "EXCELLENT for UAE summer! Tropical Asian vegetable that loves extreme heat. Very large fruits (5-20 kg) with exceptional storage life (6-12 months with waxy coating). Popular in Asian cuisine. Young fruits used like squash, mature fruits for curries/preserves. Requires strong support for heavy fruits. High yield per plant. Growing demand in Asian communities."

## 7. BUTTERNUT SQUASH (Cucurbita moschata)

### Basic Information
- **Scientific Name:** Cucurbita moschata Duchesne
- **Common Names:** Butternut Squash, Butternut Pumpkin
- **Family:** Cucurbitaceae
- **Farm Type Compatibility:** open_field, greenhouse

### Growth Cycle
- **Germination:** 7-14 days (65°F+ soil temperature)
- **Days to Harvest:** 85-110 days from seeding
- **Total Cycle:** 95-120 days
- **Flowering to Harvest:** 50-60 days

### Yield Information
- **Yield Per Plant:** 10-20 kg (4-8 fruits per plant, 1-3 kg each)
- **Expected Waste:** 25% (seeds, skin in some preparations)

### Fertilizer Schedule
**Pre-plant:**
- Fertile, organically rich soil
- Incorporate compost or aged manure

**Growing Season:**
- Balanced fertilizer (10-10-10) at planting
- Side-dress with 5-10-10 when vines begin to run
- Additional feeding at fruit set

### Hydroponic Parameters
- **pH:** 6.0-6.8 (soil and hydroponic)
- **EC:** 2.0-2.5 mS/cm
- **Temperature:** 70-85°F (21-29°C) optimal
- **Humidity:** 50-70%
- **Light:** Full sun, 8+ hours
- **Spacing:** 3-4 feet (90-120 cm) apart, rows 6-8 feet

### Common Pests & Diseases
1. **Squash Vine Borer** (HIGH severity)
   - C. moschata has GREATER RESISTANCE than other squashes
   - Prevention: Row covers, crop rotation, resistant varieties
   - Treatment: Cut stem to remove larvae, bury stem sections to re-root

2. **Cucumber Beetles** (MEDIUM-HIGH severity)
3. **Squash Bugs** (MEDIUM severity)
   - Must be controlled early
   - Prevention: Remove egg masses, trap crops
   - Treatment: Insecticides when young nymphs present

4. **Powdery Mildew** (MEDIUM severity)
   - Some resistance in C. moschata
5. **Downy Mildew** (MEDIUM severity)
6. **Anthracnose** (MEDIUM severity)

**Note:** C. moschata exhibits greater resistance to diseases and insects than other Cucurbita species, making it more suitable for challenging climates.

### Companion Planting
- **Beneficial:** Corn, Beans, Radish, Marigolds, Nasturtiums
- **Incompatible:** Potatoes

### UAE Adaptations
- **Season:** Warm season - March to June, September to November
- **Planting Months:** March, April, May, September, October
- **Harvest Months:** June, July, August, November, December, January
- **Heat Tolerance:** HIGH (C. moschata cultivars more tolerant of hot, humid weather than other squash)
- **Water Requirement:** MEDIUM-HIGH
- **Greenhouse:** Not needed for UAE
- **Notes:** "GOOD for UAE! Butternut squash (C. moschata) is MORE heat-tolerant than other squashes, making it suitable for UAE climate. Greater disease and insect resistance. Long storage life (3-6 months). Popular in modern UAE cuisine. Can grow spring and fall seasons. Plant when soil warms to 65°F+. Excellent keeper for off-season sales."

### Economics
- **Market Price:** AED 6-10/kg
- **Labor Hours:** 0.4 hours/plant

### Quality Grading
- **Grade A:** Uniform tan color, firm, heavy, no soft spots, 1-2 kg, long neck
- **Grade B:** Minor surface blemishes, 0.8-1 kg or >2 kg
